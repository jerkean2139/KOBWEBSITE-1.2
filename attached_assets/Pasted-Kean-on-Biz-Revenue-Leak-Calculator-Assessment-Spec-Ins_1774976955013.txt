Add a “Revenue Leak Calculator” assessment to the coaching homepage at keanonbiz.com/insurance. REFERENCE: CoreTrack Assessment Structure (What We’re Modeling After)
The CoreTrack Assessment at web-production-aa10.up.railway.app uses this proven structure:
Two-Phase Flow
Phase 1: “What You’re Losing” — Pain-focused questions that accumulate a running monthly loss total
Phase 2: “What You Could Gain” — Opportunity-focused questions that calculate potential recovery
UI Layout (3-Column During Assessment)
Left sidebar: Running “Estimated Leakage” counter (dollar amount that grows with each answer), risk level badge (Low → Moderate → High → Critical), dynamic motivational/fear quotes
Center: Question card with category tag, question number (X of N), question text, helper text, 4 answer options (radio buttons) OR numeric input with Next button
Right sidebar: Progress tracker showing all categories with X/N completion, social proof testimonial (rotates), and in Phase 2: “Potential Impact” calculator showing Cost Saved + Revenue Gained + hours/week recovered + Net Monthly Swing
Key UX Mechanics
Auto-advance on radio select — clicking an answer immediately loads the next question (no Next button needed for radio questions)
Numeric inputs require Next button — for dollar/number fields, user must type a value to enable the Next button
Running total animation — the leakage counter on the left updates live with each answer, creating a visceral “watching money drain” effect
Risk level escalation — starts at “Low Risk” and escalates through Moderate → High → Critical as the total grows, with color changes (green → yellow → orange → red)
Dynamic quotes — the motivational text below the leakage counter changes based on risk level (e.g., “The cracks are starting to show” → “That’s a car payment — every single month” → “You’re funding a competitor’s growth with your waste”)
Interstitial screens — appear at ~50% completion (“You’re halfway there. $X/mo.") and at the Phase 1→2 transition ("$X/mo — That’s what’s keeping you stuck as the operator — not the CEO.”)
Social proof testimonial — appears alongside questions after ~5 questions, stays visible throughout
Progress bar — red progress bar at top fills as questions are completed
Two-step funnel indicator — shows “1: What You’re Losing” and “2: What You Could Gain” at top with checkmark when Phase 1 completes
Email gate before results — after final question, show a summary screen with leaking/potential numbers and email capture. Offer both “See My Results” (gated) and “Skip — show me the summary only” (ungated but abbreviated results)
Phase 2 has BOTH radio and numeric questions — not all Phase 2 questions are numeric inputs. Some (like automatable tasks and time-use preference) use radio buttons with auto-advance.

SCORING & CALCULATION LOGIC
Phase 1 (Leakage Calculation)
Each radio button answer (Q1–Q23) has a fixed dollar amount ($0 to $1,500)
Running total accumulates with each answer
Best-case scenario: $0/mo (all first options)
Worst-case scenario: ~$23,100/mo (all worst options)
Realistic range for target audience: $3,000–$10,000/mo
Phase 2 (Recovery Calculation)
Use the Phase 1 leakage total + Phase 2 inputs to calculate two buckets:
Cost Saved (from fixing operational gaps): - Operational leak recovery: Phase 1 total × 0.65 (conservative recovery rate with coaching — better conversations, disciplined follow-up, life pivots, objection handling, and accountability systems recover most of the leakage) - Owner time recovered: Q29 hours × Q28 owner hourly value × 4.33 weeks/month × 0.50 (50% of time redirected to CEO-level work through delegation, process, and team development) - Sum = Total Cost Saved/mo
Revenue Gained (from unlocked production): - Life policy gap: (Q26 team size × 500 estimated clients/person × life penetration gap × $800 avg premium) / 12 - Life penetration gap = difference between current % (inferred from Q9 answer) and 50% target - This represents the revenue from having every team member trained to pivot every conversation to life - New business acceleration: (Q25 × 1.25 - Q25) × Q24 / 12 - Assumes 25% increase in new policy volume through better value conversations, complimentary gap audits, same-day presentation, and disciplined follow-up - Revenue upside from Q30 answer: - “Close more deals and have better conversations” → 1.3× multiplier on Revenue Gained - “Build referral partnerships and community presence” → 1.2× multiplier on Revenue Gained - “Coach, train, and develop my team” → 1.15× multiplier on Revenue Gained - “Work on the business — strategy, growth, and planning” → 1.1× multiplier on Revenue Gained - Sum = Total Revenue Gained/mo
Hours/Week Recovered: - Owner hours: Q29 × 0.60 (coaching typically recovers 60% of wasted owner hours through team development and delegation) - Total displayed as “X hrs/week recovered”
Net Monthly Swing: - -[Phase1Leakage](red)++[Cost Saved + Revenue Gained] (green) = $[Net]/mo (green)

STATE FARM CONTEXT (Important for Copy & Positioning)
State Farm agent offices run on locked-down corporate systems. The primary CRM is eCRM (built on Salesforce). Key constraints and philosophy for the assessment:
No AI or automation language in the assessment. State Farm corporate has not broadly adopted AI tools for agents. More importantly, the real coaching gaps Jeremy addresses aren’t technology gaps — they’re people gaps. The money agents lose comes from team members who aren’t having value-based conversations, aren’t pivoting to life, aren’t handling objections, aren’t following up with discipline, and aren’t being held accountable. The assessment language should focus on: systems, processes, discipline, conversations, training, objection management, value-based selling, gap audits, life pivoting, showing up prepared, and team development.
The Pocket Coach PWA runs OUTSIDE the State Farm network. Agents access it on personal phones or a dedicated office computer set up for outside URLs. It does not integrate with eCRM (yet). The assessment should NOT promise eCRM integration. Mention Pocket Coach only as a client benefit on the results/pitch page.
Future eCRM integration path (internal knowledge, NOT for marketing): State Farm uses Salesforce + MuleSoft for third-party integrations. The path to getting approved:
Register through the State Farm B2B Portal supplier process
Get sponsored by a State Farm business unit (Agent Technology or Innovations)
Meet security, compliance, and data handling requirements
Onboard through the Coupa procurement system
This is a longer-term play — do NOT reference it in any current copy
NASFA (National Association of State Farm Agents) at nasfa.com is the agent advocacy organization — potential distribution channel for the assessment.
The assessment’s core thesis is anti-State-Farm-playbook: State Farm trains agents to blow through calls, lead with price, and play the volume game. Jeremy’s coaching shifts agents to value-based discussions, complimentary gap audits, relationship-first selling, and team accountability. The questions should expose the cost of doing it the State Farm way vs. the coaching way.

COPY TONE GUIDE
Direct and unflinching — don’t sugarcoat the losses
Insurance-specific language — use terms like “book of business,” “premium,” “life pivot,” “policies in force,” “auto/fire,” “bundling,” “gap audit,” “complimentary review”
Anti-corporate — this is NOT a State Farm assessment. Frame everything as “what State Farm doesn’t teach you.” State Farm trains volume and price. Jeremy teaches value and relationships.
Psychology-forward — reference confidence, team dynamics, culture, showing up ready to perform, not just numbers
CEO vs. Agent framing — the consistent thread is: are you running your agency or is it running you?
Value > Price — the assessment should expose the cost of leading with price instead of value. Gap audits, story-based selling, objection management — these are the revenue drivers
People over technology — the gaps aren’t tech gaps. They’re conversation gaps, discipline gaps, training gaps, accountability gaps. The money is lost in the conversations your team ISN’T having or ISN’T having well
No AI/automation language — never use “AI-powered,” “automated,” “machine learning,” “artificial intelligence” in assessment questions. Use “systems,” “processes,” “training,” “discipline,” “structure”
No banned phrases — never use “just checking in,” “circle back,” “touch base,” “let me know,” “any questions?”
