2. Blog Post Length Guidance

Forget word count as a goal.

But the Studio still needs rails.

Target range:

1,200 – 1,600 words

Hard stop at ~1,800

Under 1,000 only if the topic is surgical

Why:

The rhythm creates white space

The ideas are long-form

The sentences are short

Long-form here means:

More breaths.
Not more filler.

If it hits 1,400 words and says what needed to be said → stop.

3. SEO Keywords vs. Voice Purity

This is non-negotiable:

Voice wins. Always.

But there’s a way to do SEO without polluting tone.

Rules of engagement:

Primary keyword:

Used in:

H1

URL

Meta description

First 100 words

1–2 natural mentions after

Do not force repetition in body copy

Use:

Synonyms

Adjacent phrases

Natural language

If a sentence sounds stupid when spoken out loud → it gets cut.

Exception clause:

If skipping a keyword costs >20% ranking potential on a core pillar term, we:

Keep keyword technical

Make the sentence blunt

Accept slight awkwardness once

Never more than that.

SEO bends.
Voice doesn’t break.

4. CTA Rules for Blog Posts

Blogs are not social.

They’re entry points.

Every blog gets a CTA.
But:

It must be soft

It must be contextual

It must be earned

Allowed CTAs:

Assessment invitation

Diagnostic framing

“If this feels familiar…” style exits

Not allowed:

Hard sells

“Book a call now”

Anything that sounds like a funnel slide

Think:

A door.
Not a shove.

5. Image Prompt Style

Absolutely not corporate.

The images should feel like the book reads.

Primary style:
Cinematic / documentary

Real spaces

Real tension

Quiet moments

Slight imperfection

Examples:

Empty office at night

Founder alone at a desk

Whiteboard half erased

Messy notebook, coffee ring included

Secondary style (sparingly):
Minimal graphic diagrams
Only when explaining flow or systems

Never use:

Stock-photo smiles

Abstract tech blobs

Futuristic nonsense

People pointing at nothing

If the image looks like a SaaS homepage → reject it.
