# KeanOnBiz Design Context

## Users

### Primary: Service Business Owners (General)
- **Who**: Overwhelmed owners of service businesses (agencies, coaching practices, trades) doing $500K-$5M revenue
- **State of mind**: Skeptical of coaches, burned out from doing everything themselves, heavy, looking for someone who actually *gets* their day-to-day
- **Context**: Browsing on phone during a break, or laptop at 9pm after the day's fires are out
- **Job to be done**: Find someone credible who can help them systematize and scale without losing their soul

### Secondary: Insurance Agency Owners (/insurance funnel)
- **Who**: Specifically women-owned and women-run insurance agencies
- **State of mind**: Same overwhelm but industry-specific — drowning in renewals, team issues, pipeline gaps
- **Context**: Separate funnel experience at /insurance with its own visual identity
- **Design note**: Red/white/black theme, distinct from main KeanOnBiz but should feel like the same family

### Desired 3-Second Reaction
"This guy gets it. He's been in the trenches. He's not another talking head — he actually builds things."

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: Warm. Direct. Builder.

Jeremy is not a "guru" or "thought leader" — he's a guy who's built 13 brands, helped 100+ businesses, and can explain complex systems using metaphors a 10-year-old would understand. He's the friend who happens to be brilliant at operations and won't sugarcoat what's broken.

**Voice**: Casual but authoritative. First-person. Short sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones. Uses analogies ("your business is a machine with a clogged filter") not jargon ("optimize your operational throughput").

**Emotional goal**: The visitor should feel *lighter* after 30 seconds on the site — like someone finally understood the weight they carry and offered a clear path forward.

---

## Aesthetic Direction

### Visual Tone: "Dark Editorial with Soul"
The intersection of Gary Vee's bold dark confidence, Jay Baer's editorial sophistication, and Marie Forleo's personality-forward warmth. Dark theme that feels *inviting* not *cold*. Like a well-lit whiskey bar, not a server room.

### References (What to Take)
- **Gary Vee**: Dark mode as a power move, video-forward, no visual noise, bold typography
- **Jay Baer**: Decorative geometric elements as brand signatures, editorial layout with personality (the "Tequila" nav energy), corporate credibility without corporate *boredom*
- **Marie Forleo**: Restraint through whitespace, letting personality breathe, first-person voice in design ("Heya! I'm Marie" energy), photo as a person not a headshot
- **Lewis Howes**: Content-hub architecture, podcast grid dominance, book as hero product
- **Jenna Kutcher**: Funnel-aware design where every section has a purpose, metric-based social proof

### Anti-References (What to Avoid)
- Generic coaching sites with stock photos and "Unlock Your Potential" headers
- Corporate consulting (McKinsey-style cold grids)
- Too much "brand" not enough Jeremy — the person IS the brand
- Flashy tech startup aesthetic (glassmorphism, gradient text, glow borders)
- The current site's AI-generated feel: animated gradients, glass cards, shimmer effects

### Theme
- **Main site**: Dark mode — deep navy/charcoal base, NOT pure black. Warm enough that burned-out business owners don't feel like they walked into a tech conference.
- **/insurance funnel**: Red/white/black — bold, distinct, but shares typographic and layout DNA with main site

### Color Direction
- **Primary**: Blue family — masculine, trustworthy, but deeper and more saturated than corporate blue
- **Accent**: Warm amber/gold — used surgically for CTAs and highlights, not splashed everywhere
- **Neutrals**: Tinted toward the blue hue, never pure gray
- **Text**: Off-white on dark, never pure white (#fff)

## Design Principles

1. **Jeremy First, Brand Second** — Every design decision should surface the person. Real photos, real voice, real stories. The site should feel like Jeremy invited you into his office, not like you're browsing a SaaS landing page.

2. **Stop the Scroll** — One unforgettable moment per page. A single visual that makes someone pause. Not through visual chaos (animations, gradients, glass) but through *contrast*: a bold statement against quiet space, a striking photo against minimal type, an unexpected layout break.

3. **Weight Off, Path Forward** — The design should physically feel lighter as you scroll down. Start with the visitor's pain ("You're doing everything yourself"), transition through understanding ("Here's why"), and resolve with clarity ("Here's the path"). The visual rhythm should mirror this emotional arc.

4. **Editorial, Not Template** — Layout like a magazine, not a WordPress theme. Vary section structures. Asymmetric grids. Pull quotes. Full-bleed photography next to tight text columns. Break the pattern intentionally.

5. **Earn Every Element** — If it doesn't serve the visitor's journey toward "I need to talk to this guy," remove it. No decorative cards, no sparklines, no animated backgrounds. Every pixel earns its keep.
