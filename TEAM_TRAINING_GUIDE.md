## The Big Picture

Our website isn't just a brochure—it's a **lead generation machine** with **automated content creation**.

**Three main goals:**
1. **Attract** visitors with valuable content (blog posts, podcasts, free tools)
2. **Convert** them into leads through forms, assessments, and workshops
3. **Nurture** them with automated follow-up until they book a call

Think of it like a funnel:

```
                 ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                 │        BLOG / CONTENT           │  ← Visitors find us
                 └───────────────┬─────────────────┘
                                 ▼
                 ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                 │    ASSESSMENT / WORKSHOP        │  ← They engage
                 └───────────────┬─────────────────┘
                                 ▼
                 ┌─────────────────────────────────┐

                                 ▼
                 ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
                 │       BOOK A CALL               │  ← They convert!
                 └─────────────────────────────────┘
```

## The Content Suite

Our website has powerful admin tools that create and manage content using AI. These are all password-protected.

### Why We Built This

Creating content is time-consuming. A single blog post can take 4-6 hours. A newsletter might take a full day. A podcast episode requires scripting, recording, editing.

Our Content Suite uses AI to:
- **Research** trending topics and real industry pain points automatically
- **Write** in Jeremy's voice (using his specific vocabulary and style)
- **Generate** professional images and podcast audio
- **Optimize** for SEO and search engines
- **Review** everything before publishing

This lets us publish high-quality content daily instead of weekly.

Go to the **Generate** tab and fill in:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Topic / Theme** | What the episode is about (e.g., "Why your best employee is your biggest risk") |
| **Target Length** | 5 minutes, 7 minutes, or 10 minutes |

```
│                  │ Episode saved with script, ready for editing │
└──────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Step 2: Review and Edit the Script

Go to the **Episodes** tab. Click any episode to view it.

- **View** — Read the full script, see episode metadata
- **Edit** — Click "Edit" to modify the title, description, and script text
- **Save** — Click "Save" after making changes

#### Step 3: Synthesize Audio (ElevenLabs)

Once the script is ready:

1. In the episode view, find the **Voice Synthesis** section
2. Configure the **Voice ID** (Jeremy's cloned voice) and **Model** (e.g., eleven_multilingual_v2)
3. Click **"Generate Audio"**
4. The AI converts the script to spoken audio using ElevenLabs
5. Audio is saved to the server and an **audio player** appears for preview
6. If the script changes, click **"Generate Audio"** again to regenerate

#### Step 4: Approve & Publish

Go to the **Approval** tab:

1. Select an episode from the draft queue
2. Review the **Quality Checklist**:

| Check | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| Title present | Episode has a title |
| Description > 50 chars | Meaningful description written |
| Script > 500 words | Full script generated |
| Audio generated | ElevenLabs audio file exists |
| Duration > 0 | Audio has measurable length |
| Topics assigned | Episode tagged with topics |
| Transcript present | Text transcript available |

3. Preview the script and listen to the audio player
4. If everything looks good, click **"Approve & Publish"**
5. Status changes: Draft → Published, and `publishedAt` is set
6. The episode appears on the public Podcast page and in the RSS feed

To go back and edit, click **"Edit Script"** to return to the Episodes tab.

### RSS Feed

Published episodes are automatically included in the podcast RSS feed at `/podcast.xml`. This feed is compatible with Apple Podcasts, Spotify, and other podcast directories.

### Episode Management

From the Episodes tab you can:
- View all episodes (draft, approved, published)
- Edit any episode's title, description, and script
- Delete episodes
- See episode metadata (episode number, duration, creation date)

## Industry Intelligence

**URL:** `/admin/pain-points`

**What it does:** Manages a database of real industry pain points that feed into Content Studio, Newsletter Creator, and MicroPod Studio. Includes automated weekly research to discover new pain points from the web.

### Getting Started

1. Go to `/admin/pain-points`
2. Enter your admin key
3. You'll see tabs: Dashboard, Industries, Pain Points, and Research

### Why This Matters

Instead of writing generic content, our AI tools pull **real pain points** from this database. When Content Studio generates a blog post targeting Insurance Agencies, it pulls actual pain points like "manual policy renewals" and "CSR turnover" to make the content specific and resonant.

### The Dashboard

Overview statistics at a glance:
- **Industries** — Number of tracked industries
- **Pain Points** — Total pain points in the database
- **Research Runs** — Number of automated research cycles completed
- **Last Research** — Date of the most recent research run

Charts show:
- **Pain Points per Industry** — Bar chart of coverage by industry
- **Category Distribution** — How pain points break down by category (operations, sales, marketing, tech, hiring)
- **Coverage Gaps** — Industries with fewer than 8 pain points that need attention

### Managing Industries

Go to the **Industries** tab:

#### Adding an Industry
1. Click **"Add Industry"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Name** — e.g., "Insurance Agencies"
   - **Slug** — Auto-generated URL-friendly name
   - **Description** — What this industry encompasses
   - **Target Persona** — Who we're targeting (e.g., "Agency owners with 5-50 employees")
   - **Status** — Active or Inactive
3. Click **Save**

#### Editing/Deleting
- Click the **edit icon** on any industry to modify it
- Click the **trash icon** to delete an industry and all its pain points

### The 7 Target Industries (Pre-Seeded)

The database comes pre-loaded with 7 industries, each with 8-12 pain points:

| Industry | Example Pain Points |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Insurance Agencies** | Carrier appointment delays, manual policy renewals, CSR turnover, commission tracking |
| **Business Coaches/Consultants** | Client implementation gap, scaling past 1:1, lead gen inconsistency, no onboarding system |
| **Marketing Agencies** | Scope creep, client reporting overhead, campaign attribution, retainer churn |
| **Real Estate Agents** | Lead follow-up gaps, CRM data rot, post-close nurture, showing scheduling chaos |
| **Title Companies** | Manual document chasing, compliance tracking, title search delays, fee calculation errors |
| **Small Business Owners** | Wearing all hats, cash flow unpredictability, no documented processes, delegation fear |
| **Home Service Companies** | Scheduling/dispatch chaos, estimate-to-invoice gaps, seasonal demand swings, review management |

To seed the database for the first time, click **"Seed Database"** on the Dashboard tab.

### Managing Pain Points

Go to the **Pain Points** tab:

#### Filtering
- **By Industry** — Dropdown to show only one industry's pain points
- **By Category** — Filter by operations, sales, marketing, tech, or hiring
- **Search** — Search by keyword, title, or description

#### Adding a Pain Point
1. Click **"Add Pain Point"**
2. Fill in:
   - **Industry** — Which industry this belongs to
   - **Category** — Operations, Sales, Marketing, Tech, or Hiring
   - **Title** — Short name (e.g., "Manual Policy Renewals")
   - **Description** — Detailed explanation of the pain point
   - **Severity** — Score from 1-10 (10 = most painful)
   - **Source** — "Manual" (you added it) or "Research" (automated discovery)
   - **Source URL** — Link to where you found it (optional)
   - **Keywords** — Comma-separated keywords for content matching
   - **Manumation Angle** — How Jeremy/Manumation approach solves this problem
3. Click **Save**

#### Editing/Deleting
- Click the **edit icon** on any pain point to modify it
- Click the **trash icon** to delete it

### How Research Runs Work

Go to the **Research** tab:

#### Automated Weekly Research
The system runs automated research every Monday at 6am:
1. For each active industry, it builds targeted search queries like:
   - "[industry] biggest challenges 2026"
   - "[industry] owners struggling with"
   - "[industry] automation pain points"
2. Web search fetches results from news, trade sites, and forums
3. GPT-4o analyzes findings and extracts new pain points
4. AI categorizes each finding, scores severity, and writes a manumation angle
5. Deduplicates against existing pain points using keyword matching
6. New findings are saved with source URLs and tagged as "research"

#### Manual Research Trigger
Click **"Run Research Now"** to trigger a research run manually. Results appear in the research history below.

#### Research History
Each run shows:
- **Date** — When the research ran
- **Industries Searched** — Which industries were included
- **Total Findings** — How many potential pain points were found
- **New Added** — How many new (non-duplicate) pain points were saved
- **Status** — Running, Completed, or Failed
- **Log** — Click to see detailed log messages from the run

## The Waterfall Workshop

**URL:** `/waterfall-workshop`

**What it is:** A free interactive tool that helps business owners identify tasks they should delegate.

### How It Works

#### Phase 1: Brain Dump
Users list everything on their plate—all the tasks taking up their time.

#### Phase 2: Sort Tasks
They drag each task into one of three columns:
- **Only I Can Do** - Tasks requiring their unique expertise
- **Delegate Soon** - Tasks that could be handed off eventually
- **Delegate NOW** - Tasks that should be delegated immediately

#### Phase 3: Prioritize
For "Delegate NOW" tasks, they can mark:
- **Priority Flag** - Most urgent
- **Operations Flag** - Operational/recurring

#### Phase 4: Action Plan
They get a summary showing:
- Total tasks identified
- Breakdown by category
- Recommended next steps

### Why This Tool Is Powerful

1. **Self-Qualification** - People who complete it have proven they need help
2. **Specific Pain Points** - We know exactly what they struggle with
3. **Investment** - They've spent time with us (high engagement)
4. **Natural Next Step** - Leads directly to booking a coaching call

### Donna AI Assistant

During the workshop, users can chat with "Donna"—an AI assistant powered by OpenAI's GPT-4o. Donna helps them:
- Think through which category each task belongs in
- Challenge assumptions about "only I can do" tasks
- Suggest delegation strategies
